ISO 13616 (International Bank Account Number - IBAN)

Primary Purpose: Identifies individual bank accounts

Level of Identification: Account level (specific account)

Purpose:

  • Account Identification: ISO 13616 defines the International Bank Account Number (IBAN), which is used to identify individual bank accounts across borders.
  • Error Reduction: The IBAN format helps reduce errors in international transactions by providing a standard way to represent bank account numbers.

Key Features:

  • Format: An IBAN consists of up to 34 alphanumeric characters, including:
    • A country code (2 letters)
    • A check digit (2 digits)
    • A Basic Bank Account Number (BBAN), which varies by country and represents the domestic account number along with additional routing information.

Example:

  • An IBAN might look like DE89370400440532013000, which represents a specific account in Germany.
